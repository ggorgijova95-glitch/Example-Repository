Book geners description

  • Fantasy

    Fantasy
    Stories set in imaginary worlds with magic, mythical creatures, and epic adventures.
    1. Harry Potter and the Sorcerers Stone
    2. The Hobbit
    3. The Name of the Wind
    4. Mistborn
    5. A Game of Thrones

  • Science Fiction

    Science Fiction
    Fiction based on futuristic concepts, advanced technology, space exploration, and often explores the impact of science on society.
    1. Dune
    2. 1984
    3. The Martian
    4. Enders Game
    5. Brave New World

  • Mystery

    Mystery
    Fiction that involves solving a crime or uncovering secrets, often featuring detectives or amateur sleuths.
    1. The Girl on the Train
    2. Sherlock Holmes: The Hound of the Baskervilles
    3. Gone Girl
    4. The Da Vinci Code
    5. And Then There Were None

  • Romance

    Romance
    Fiction that focuses on romantic relationships between characters, often with emotional and passionate storylines.
    1. Pride and Prejudice
    2. Me Before You
    3. The Notebook
    4. Outlander
    5. The Fault in Our Stars

  • Horror

    Horror
    Fiction designed to evoke fear, dread, and suspense, often featuring supernatural elements or psychological terror.
    1. Dracula
    2. It
    3. The Haunting of Hill House
    4. Bird Box
    5. The Exorcist

  • Non-Fiction

    Non-Fiction
    Factual books that provide information, insights, or narratives based on real events, people, or concepts.
    1. Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind
    2. Educated
    3. The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks
    4. Becoming
    5. The Wright Brothers

  • Historical Fiction

    Historical Fiction
    Fiction set in a specific historical period, often blending real historical events and figures with fictional characters and narratives.
    1. The Book Thief
    2. All the Light We Cannot See
    3. The Nightingale
    4. The Tattooist of Auschwitz
    5. The Pillars of the Earth

  • Trailler

    Trailler
    Fiction characterized by fast-paced plots, suspenseful situations, and high stakes, often involving crime, espionage, or action-packed scenarios.
    1. The Silence of the Lambs
    2. The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo
    3. The Shining
    4. Shutter Island
    5. The Bourne Identity

  • Young Adult

    Young Adult
    Fiction targeted at teenage readers, often dealing with themes of identity, coming of age, and personal growth.
    1. The Hunger Games
    2. Divergent
    3. The Fault in Our Stars
    4. The Maze Runner
    5. Eleanor & Park

  • Biography

    Biography
    A detailed description of a person's life, often highlighting their achievements, challenges, and impact on society.
    1. Becoming
    2. Steve Jobs
    3. Educated
    4. The Diary of a Young Girl
    5. Long Walk to Freedom

  • Self-help

    Self-Help
    Books that provide guidance, strategies, and advice for personal development, improving mental health, and achieving goals.
    1. Atomic Habits
    2. The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck
    3. The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People
    4. How to Win Friends and Influence People
    5. Think and Grow Rich
